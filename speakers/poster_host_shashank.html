The Inter-disciplinary Centre for Quantum Computing (ICQC) at the Indian Institute of Technology Indore is dedicated to advancing state-of-the-art research in quantum technologies and establishing an industry-ready workforce for India's growing quantum ecosystem.
Dr. Shashank Gupta
Assistant Professor
Physics & ICQC, IIT Indore
Host Bio

Dr. Shashank Gupta is an Assistant Professor at the Centre for Interdisciplinary Area of Quantum Computing and Department of Physics, IIT Indore. He is also an Innovation and Academy consultant at QNu Labs. He has an Integrated PhD in Theoretical Physics (2015-21) from S. N. Bose National Centre for Basic Sciences, Kolkata, India. He has worked on the foundations and applications of multipartite quantum resources during his PhD tenure. He then joined QNu Labs, an Indian start-up working in Quantum Cryptography, as a Senior Research Associate (2021-23) and as a Research Lead (2024-25). He and a dedicated team of engineers worked on breaking distance barriers in quantum key distribution, parallelly creating novel quantum-safe solutions for the post-quantum era. He also served as a post-doctoral researcher at the Applied Cryptography Unit at OIST, Japan, led by Prof. Carlos Cid. His research interests include but are not confined to quantum multi-party computation on quantum networks, quantum simulators for quantum communications, quantum cryptography, and post-quantum cryptography. Besides research, he is driven towards investing, sketching, and deciphering.

Key Highlights:
  • Current Roles: Assistant Professor at Physics & ICQC, IIT Indore; Innovation consultant at QNu Labs.
  • Education: Integrated PhD in Theoretical Physics from S.N. Bose National Centre (2015-21).
  • Industry Experience: Research Lead & Sr. Research Associate at QNu Labs (2021-25), advancing quantum key distribution.
  • Global Research: Post-doctoral researcher at the Applied Cryptography Unit, OIST Japan.
  • Expertise: Quantum cryptography, post-quantum cryptography, and quantum multi-party networks.
